- Allers et al. (2011). Evidence for the cure of HIV infection by CCR5Δ32/Δ32 stem cell transplantation. Blood.
- Yukl et al. (2013). Challenges in detecting HIV persistence during potentially curative interventions: a study of the Berlin patient. PLoS Pathog.

Clinical Sciences

- Barsky (2017). The iatrogenic potential of the physician’s words. JAMA.

Epidemiology

- Braithwaite (2013). EBM’s six dangerous words. JAMA.
- Ioannidis (2016). Evidence-based medicine has been hijacked: A report to David Sackett. J Clin Epidemiol.
- Nunan et al. (2017). Ten essential papers for the practice of evidence-based medicine. Evid Based Med.

Philosophy

Rationalism

- Kelly (2018). The need for a rationalist turn in evidence‐based medicine. J Eval Clin Pract.
- Djulbegovic et al. (2018). Rational decision making in medicine: Implications for overuse and underuse. J Eval Clin Pract.

Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
An incredible resource for learning and studying philosophy from outside the field, with a number of entries relevant to the study of medicine.

- Reiss & Ankeny (2016). Philosophy of medicine.
- DeGrazia (2017). The definition of death.
- Eyal (2012). Informed consent.
- Archard (2016). Children’s rights.

Statistics

Introductions

- Motulsky (2017). Intuitive Biostatistics. A nonmathematical guide to statistical thinking. Oxford University Press.
- Kunin et al. (2018). Seeing Theory. Brown University.

Data Visualization

- Wagenmakers & Gronau (2018). A compendium of clean graphs in R.

Misconceptions
This article from a group of prominent statisticians and epidemiologists provides an extensive list of common and subtle misconceptions about foundational concepts in orthodox statistics.

- Greenland et al. (2016). Statistical tests, P values, confidence intervals, and power: A guide to misinterpretations. Eur J Epidemiol.

Confidence Intervals
This sequence of four articles in the Psychonomic Bulletin & Review explores the common understanding, underlying theory, and shortcomings in applicability of classical confidence intervals.

- Hoekstra et al. (2014). Robust misinterpretation of confidence intervals.
- Miller & Ulrich (2016). Interpreting confidence intervals: A comment on Hoekstra, Morey, Rouder, and Wagenmakers (2014).
- Morey et al. (2016). Continued misinterpretation of confidence intervals: response to Miller and Ulrich.
- Morey et al. (2016). The fallacy of placing confidence in confidence intervals.

Bayesian Inference

- Wagenmakers et al. (2016). Bayesian benefits for the pragmatic researcher. Curr Dir Psychol Sci.
- Hoekstra et al. (2018). Bayesian reanalysis of null results reported in medicine: Strong yet variable evidence for the absence of treatment effects. PLoS ONE.
